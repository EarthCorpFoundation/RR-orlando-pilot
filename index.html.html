Indoor Urban Farm (IUF) — Designated first EPF recipient of a fully operational Orlando RR program. Independently operated; EarthCorp designs the structure and retains participation rights via the EarthFood model. Produces greens, herbs, and microgreens for pilot-zone restaurants and retailers — closing the geographic loop. Facility to be proposed as a state or city-donated or nominally-leased building in the Orlando vicinity.
Domestic worker model — Regeneration Work Credit (RWC)
IUF workers in the US are compensated through conventional USD payroll. Their labor is attested via WAU and passes dual-layer consensus, but resulting U-PIPs flow into the international pool for global poverty uplift — consistent with the Doctrine's Dual Economy Model. US workers receive a Regeneration Work Credit (RWC): a non-monetary, verifiable attestation credential with no fiat redemption pathway, generating no tax event. EPF funds IUF labor costs until the IUF reaches operational self-sufficiency.
Proof-of-concept thresholds

Four validation categories — all must be met

Behavioral adoption
Members complete at least one qualifying transaction
Minimum: 40% activation within 90 days
Retailer integration
DDV operational at point of sale
Minimum: 25 retailers confirmed & transacting
Capital generation
Verified EPF balance generated
Minimum: Any non-zero verified EPF balance
Verification function
ESA proxy audits + IUF labor attestation
100% of retailers audited · ≥1 IUF WAU generated

Partial validation in one category does not constitute proof of concept. Success requires minimum thresholds across all four. Pilot uses simplified proxy technology stack — behavioral and market proof only, not patent-spec technical deployment.
